Bioinformatic Analyst

A bioinformatics analyst deals with huge databases of omics data, such as the Human Genome Project's genomics investigations. In this profession, you'll conduct study into the pathology of diseases as well as devise tests and algorithms to locate solutions.

Bioinformatic Scientist

Bioinformatics scientists use technology and computer science to study and find solutions in the area of biology. They use databases of genetic information to find ways to identify and treat human, animal and plant diseases and other issues.

Research Associate

In a biology or life sciences research lab, a bioinformatics associate's job is to undertake detailed data analysis. They're in charge of gathering data through primary research and constructing models with the use of a data algorithm or other analytical tools.

Bioinformatics Software Developer

Bioinformatics software engineers study human genetic makeup and develop software that organises, visualises, and stores the information they discover. They study cell activity in order to develop new drugs and therapies for a variety of ailments.

Junior Research Fellow

A junior bioinformatician's job include analysing and sequencing data using open-source software. He or she must create sequencing data analysis pipelines.

Bioinformatics Technician

They apply bioinformatics tools to visualize, analyze, manipulate or interpret molecular data.
